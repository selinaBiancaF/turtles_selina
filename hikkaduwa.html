Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The sea tutle hatchery aspires to safeguard and nurture these endangered turtles that visitd the golden shores of Hikkaduwa.

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre is located a few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town, with the goal of protecting some of the world's most endangered turtle species. It is one of several turtle hatcheries on the island's southern coast. It began as a community project to help maintain the biodiversity of the southern coast and has since grown into a full-fledged hatchery and rescue center with dedicated staff working around the clock to study, care for, and protect many turtle species. The sea turtle hatchery and rescue center's facilities are cutting-edge, with water tanks to care for newborn turtles as well as adult turtle species rescued from the sea due to injuries. The place where the turtles lay their eggs is one of the more unusual views in the center, since it has distinct small heaps of sand piled neatly between one another. Each of these heaps is continuously watched in order to compute the hatching of the turtle eggs. what you can do in the turtle hatchery?Release baby turtles to the sea,Feed the sea turtles ,Collect eggs from the shore,Turtle watching and Night patrols.

Visiting hours:8.00am to 6.00pm
