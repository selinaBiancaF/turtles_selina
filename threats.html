Threats to Turtles


Sea turtles come in seven different species. Six of them are threatened or endangered due to human behavior or way of life. The following are a few of the greatest dangers to turtles:


1. Entanglement in fishing gear: The biggest threat to sea turtles and a lot of other marine animals today is accidental capture in fishing nets and lines. About 40% of the captured animals are thrown away as trash. Tens of miles long longlines with thousands of baited hooks can catch countless unintentional victims.This can be prevented by using fishing gears which are larger than the size of turtles.


2. Illegal Poaching:Despite legislation protecting sea turtles in many countries, illegal poaching is still a major issue. This includes the trafficking in their eggs, meat, and shells. The skin and shell are used to produce jewelry, eyewear, and other items, while the flesh and eggs are consumed. This can be prevented by implementing illiberal rule on fisherman.
